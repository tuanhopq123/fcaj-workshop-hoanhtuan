## Overall Evaluation

### 1. Learning Environment

The learning environment throughout the internship was highly supportive and well organized. The internship roadmap clearly defined weekly objectives, allowing me to gradually improve my AWS knowledge from fundamental concepts to designing a complete cloud-native solution.

The combination of project implementation and AWS hands-on labs made the learning experience practical instead of purely theoretical.

---

### 2. Support from Mentors and Community

The mentors and community members were always willing to answer technical questions whenever I encountered difficulties.

Instead of providing ready-made solutions, they usually guided me toward official AWS documentation and encouraged independent problem solving. This approach significantly improved my ability to analyze problems and search for appropriate technical solutions.

---

### 3. Relevance to My Academic Major

The internship closely matched my Software Engineering background.

Many concepts learned at university, including software architecture, databases, RESTful APIs, and software development principles, were directly applied while building the Smart Home Energy Waste Monitoring & Alert System on AWS.

At the same time, the internship introduced many modern cloud technologies that are not deeply covered in university courses.

---

### 4. Learning and Professional Development

During the internship, I gained practical experience with many AWS services, including:

- AWS Lambda
- Amazon API Gateway
- Amazon DynamoDB
- Amazon S3
- Amazon CloudWatch
- Amazon IAM
- AWS KMS
- AWS CloudTrail
- Amazon RDS
- AWS Glue
- Amazon Athena
- Amazon QuickSight

Besides technical knowledge, I also improved my documentation writing, system architecture design, debugging, and cloud cost optimization skills following the AWS Well-Architected Framework.

---

### 5. Internship Experience

One aspect I appreciated most was that the internship combined theory with extensive hands-on practice.

Instead of only reading documentation, I had opportunities to deploy complete AWS infrastructures, troubleshoot real deployment issues, optimize system performance, and progressively improve my project over twelve weeks.

Participating in FCAJ Community Day also broadened my understanding of current cloud computing and AI industry trends.

---

### 6. Overall Satisfaction

Overall, I am very satisfied with the internship program.

The internship not only strengthened my AWS technical skills but also improved my confidence in designing cloud architectures and solving real-world engineering problems.

---

## Additional Questions

### What did you enjoy the most during your internship?

The most valuable experience was building a complete cloud-native application from scratch while gradually improving it into a production-oriented architecture.

This allowed me to understand not only individual AWS services but also how they work together in a real system.

---

### What could be improved?

Providing additional advanced workshops focusing on architecture design, CI/CD, Infrastructure as Code (Terraform or CloudFormation), and real production case studies would make the internship even more beneficial.

---

### Would you recommend this internship?

Yes.

I would highly recommend this internship to students who want practical AWS experience because it provides a structured learning roadmap, numerous hands-on labs, and valuable opportunities to build real cloud projects.

---

## Suggestions and Expectations

I hope the program will continue expanding its practical learning materials and organize more technical sharing sessions from AWS professionals.

If given the opportunity, I would be very interested in participating in future advanced training programs related to Cloud Architecture, DevOps, and Artificial Intelligence on AWS.

Overall, this internship has become an important milestone in my learning journey and has provided a solid foundation for my future career in Cloud Computing and Software Engineering.
